# Other noises (turning/accelerating/constant) — Perplexity research (raw, Prompt 2)

Status: usable for domain/playbooks.ts. One band-vocabulary defect noted at bottom.

## Question flow

1. **When does the noise happen?** Only when turning · Only while accelerating · Constant,
   rises/falls with speed · Only when coasting/decelerating.
2. **Where (if you can tell)?** Front L/R/both · Rear L/R/both · Under car/center/engine bay/
   near axle · Not sure.
3. **What does it sound like?** Hum/growl/rumble · Whine/high-pitched whirr · Click/pop ·
   Clunk/knock · Squeal/chirp · Metallic grind.
4. **Steering direction effect?** Louder turning left · louder turning right · louder
   straightening · no change.
5. **Any associated feel?** Vibration in wheel/floor · steering loose/heavy · shudder under
   accel · fluid leak/smell/smoke · gear/shift problems or warning lights.

## Decision tree

### A. Turning-only hum/growl, changes with steer → wheel bearing
Likely causes: failing wheel bearing/hub (70%), tire cupping mimicking hum (15%), CV joint/axle
(10%), loose suspension component (5%).
Urgency: **book-soon** → **fix-now** if vibration/looseness or hot hub.
Cost per wheel — DIY: $60–250 · Indie: $150–450 · Dealer: $250–800+.
Escalate: steering play, loud/gritty increase over a short drive, hub very hot, ABS/stability
light on.
Shop-brief: "Speed-proportional hum that changes with turning, suspect a failing wheel
bearing/hub on the [L/R] side; please check wheel bearing play, hub temperature after a short
drive, and ABS sensor wheel speed readings."

### B. Click/pop when turning, front → outer CV joint
Likely causes: outer CV joint failing (75%), torn CV boot/grease loss (15%), steering/ball joint
or suspension play (7%), wheel bearing (3%).
Urgency: **book-soon** (short drives OK) → **fix-now** if clicking becomes continuous or severe
vibration.
Cost per axle — DIY: $80–300 (axle) or $40–120 (boot/grease if caught early) · Indie: $250–700
(range up to $1,100) · Dealer: $500–1,400+.
Escalate: clicking → constant knocking, vibration under accel, grease sprayed in wheel well,
sudden loss of drive.
Shop-brief: "Sharp clicking on turns at low speed; suspect outer CV joint/axle on the [L/R]
side — please inspect CV boot, joint play, confirm axle vs boot/grease service."

### C. Constant whine/whirr with road speed, not throttle-linked → differential/driveline
Likely causes: worn differential or transfer-case bearings (35%), transmission/driveshaft
carrier bearing (25%), wheel bearing if localized (20%), tire noise/imbalance (15%), loose heat
shield/underbody resonance (5%).
Urgency: **book-soon** → **fix-now** if noise sharpens quickly, gear grinding, or drivability
loss.
Cost — DIY: $40–800 (many jobs not DIY) · Indie: $300–1,800 · Dealer: $800–2,500+.
Escalate: rapid volume/roughness increase, grinding, burning smell, drivetrain warning lights,
sudden loss of drive or gear pop-out.
Shop-brief: "Constant speed-proportional whine across [range], not RPM-linked — please
road-test, confirm coast vs throttle behavior, check differential/transmission fluid, inspect
pinion/output bearings."

### D. Whine/hum rising with engine RPM → accessory/transmission
Likely causes: transmission/torque-converter bearing or pump (35%), accessory drive (power
steering/alternator/AC) under load (30%), engine bearing or belt idler/tensioner (25%), vacuum
pump/supercharger if equipped (10%).
Urgency: **book-soon** → **fix-now** if sudden harsh metal noise, loss of power-steering assist,
or leaks. (Note: source used a non-standard "fix-soon" label here — treat as book-soon with a
tight window, see caveat below.)
Cost — DIY: $30–300 (accessory parts) · Indie: $150–900 · Dealer: $300–1,500+.
Escalate: sudden loss of power steering assist, visible fluid leak, change in gear engagement.
Shop-brief: "RPM-correlated whine; please isolate whether it tracks engine RPM in neutral vs in
gear, check accessory drive components, belts/tensioners, and transmission bearings."

### E. Clunk/knock under acceleration → driveline lash
Likely causes: worn U-joint/driveshaft center bearing (35%), faulty engine/transmission mounts
(30%), loose differential mount/bushings (20%), axle/hub play or CV joint if front (15%).
Urgency: **book-soon** → **fix-now** if clunking escalates, causes vibration, or affects safe
control.
Cost — DIY: $30–200 (mounts/U-joint kits) · Indie: $150–700 · Dealer: $300–1,200+.
Escalate: sudden shift to continuous knocking, large vibration/shudder, drivetrain binding.
Shop-brief: "Intermittent clunk on acceleration; please inspect U-joints/driveshaft center
bearing, engine/transmission mounts, differential mounting bushings — report which shows play or
visual failure."

### F. Squeal/chirp under acceleration → belt/pulley/accessory
Likely causes: worn/slipping serpentine belt (40%), idler/tensioner bearing or pulley failing
(30%), accessory pulley bearing (20%), misalignment/oil contamination (10%).
Urgency: **can-coast**/**book-soon** depending on persistence → **fix-now** if losing charging or
power steering.
Cost — DIY: $20–60 (belt), $30–150 (tensioner/pulley) · Indie: $80–300 · Dealer: $150–500+.
Escalate: battery light/charging issues, loss of power steering assist, visible belt fraying or
rapidly worsening squeal.
Shop-brief: "High-pitch squeal on start/accel; please inspect serpentine belt condition/
tensioner/pulleys and alternator/power-steering for bearing noise; confirm belt vs pulley
replacement."

### G. Rumble/hum at certain speeds → tires/wheels
Likely causes: cupped/scalloped tire wear or defect (50%), wheel imbalance/bent rim (20%), wheel
bearing if localized (15%), strut mount/bushing resonance (15%).
Urgency: **book-soon** → **fix-now** if vibration increases or tread severely worn.
Cost per tire — DIY: $50–200 · Indie: $80–250 (+ balancing $15–50/wheel) · Dealer: $100–400
installed.
Escalate: rapid vibration growth, visible tire cords, bulge, tread separation.
Shop-brief: "Speed-dependent rumble that may track with road surface — please inspect tread for
cupping/scalloping, check wheel balance, confirm rim straightness and tire pressure; recommend
road-force balance if needed."

## General escalation triggers (any branch → fix-now)
Steering looseness/play · sudden strong vibration · grinding metallic noise · smoke or burning
smell · pulling that affects control · dramatic noise increase over a short drive · sudden fluid
leaks · drivetrain engagement failure.

**Caveats (from review, not the source):**
1. Branch D used the label "fix-soon," which isn't in the app's 4-band vocabulary (fix-now /
   book-soon / can-coast / all-clear) — read it as book-soon with a short window when coding
   this branch; don't add a 5th band.
2. Same sourcing pattern as prompt 1: shop-pricing/consumer blogs, not NHTSA complaint data —
   treat likelihood percentages as ranking heuristics, not measured stats; soften to "most often"
   in UI copy.
3. This response is denser than the other 3 playbooks (7 branches vs. the plan's simpler scope) —
   fine to keep all 7, or compress at implementation time; nothing here needs re-asking.
