## Overall Impression

The verdict layer is genuinely excellent and the honest-unknown state is designed rather than bolted on — `VerdictPanel.tsx:74` *removes* the meter for an unknown vehicle instead of showing an empty track, with the reasoning written into the file. That is real design judgment.

The problem is that this discipline stops at the narration layer. One layer down, the ledger renders green rows, green pills, and green lamps for services that never happened, and the input predicate that gates the whole Earned Green Rule accepts an odometer reading as evidence of history. The app's single most important promise is broken by its most common onboarding path.

**Biggest opportunity:** thread `known`/`hasServiceHistory` all the way down to the row renderers. `VehicleDetail.tsx:168` already computes it. It's a four-line fix that turns the app's headline principle from aspirational to true.

## What's Working

1. **The honest-unknown state is suppressive, not additive.** `VerdictPanel.tsx:38/74` removes the ruler and the meter for an unknown vehicle rather than showing them at zero. `verdict.ts:88-90` types `rulerPin` as `number | null` so an unknown vehicle is unrepresentable-as-a-position at the type level. Most teams ship a gray bar at 0%; a neutral instrument reads as a low score, absence of an instrument does not.
2. **Urgency has exactly one source of truth.** `verdict.ts:7-10` and `health.ts:10-11` both declare the verdict layer a pure re-narration forbidden from re-deciding. `playbooks.ts:1345-1358` returns an identity-equal object on no-op and only ever prepends text — it "can sharpen a verdict but never relax one." This is what makes the four-band vocabulary learnable in one session.
3. **Copy discipline enforced in the domain layer, not left to writers.** `playbooks.ts:17-19` discards the source research's percentages because "showing a precise '70%' would overstate certainty." `verdict.ts:100` renders due dates at month precision because "verdicts promise windows, not days." `quoteCheck.ts:10-13` caps the worst label at "worth a second look, not fraud." Each is a decision to be less impressive in order to be more trustworthy.

## Priority Issues

### [P0] "All clear. Just drive." on a car with zero service history
**Why it matters:** This is the one thing PRODUCT.md promises not to do, in the scenario the product exists for — someone who just bought a used car with unknown history. `Vehicles.tsx:50`'s "Completed" pill isn't merely optimistic tone; it's a false factual claim about work that never happened.
**Fix:** (1) Split the predicate — `hasServiceHistory` = `r.lastDone.date != null || r.lastDone.miles != null`, dropping the `milesRemaining` clause; keep `hasRealData` for odometer-confidence only. (2) `reminderEngine.ts:243-245` must never return `'completed'` from the never-serviced branch — clamp to `'watch-next'`. (3) Guard `Vehicles.tsx:49` and `VehicleDetail.tsx`'s row renderer: a row with no `lastDone` renders a slate "Not tracked yet" pill. (4) Use band vocabulary, not engine vocabulary, on Vehicles.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden`

## Questions to Consider

1. **If `hasRealData` returning true for a bare odometer reading is a bug, what is an odometer reading actually evidence of?** The product has conflated two orthogonal axes: do we know *where* this car is (odometer) vs. do we know *what's been done to it* (history). The four-signal band answers the second; the confidence note answers the first. Right now the first silently authorizes the second.
2. **The Earned Green Rule is enforced beautifully at the narration layer and nowhere at the ledger layer. Is that a bug, or an unstated belief that the schedule is "engine truth" exempt from the brand's honesty rules?** `VehicleDetail.tsx:168` computes `known` and uses it for one sentence while nine rows below render green. Someone knew the value was needed and chose not to thread it through. If the ledger is exempt, say so in DESIGN.md.
3. **Does the Urgency Ruler earn its place, or is it the app's most beautiful redundancy?** The panel already states the band in words, in color, and in a sentence. DESIGN.md:185 pre-authorizes the pairing as "the brand" — so which document wins, the principle or the exemption?
4. **Check renders every layer of depth expanded. Was "depth on demand" abandoned there because collapsing safety information felt irresponsible?** If so that's defensible and should be written down as an explicit exception. If not, the highest-stress screen is the one that most violates the principle.
